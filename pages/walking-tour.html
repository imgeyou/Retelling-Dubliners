Navigating Dublin

Frequently asked questions about Dublin's public transport system.

01 What are the main types of public transport in Dublin Centre?

Dublin's public transport network includes buses, trams, trains, and bike sharing schemes. Routes, maps, planning a journey, ticketing, real-time traffic and travel updates can all be found at TransportforIreland.


DublinBikes is a self-service rental system open to everyone aged 14 and up. Users must purchase a ticket (1-day, 3-day, or annual subscription) to access the service. Within each ticket, the first 30 minutes of every individual journey are free. You can view nearby stations and available bikes via the official RedClick dublinbikes app or at a station terminal. Payment is via TFI Leap Card or a dublinbikes card. Bleeper and MOBY Bikes are two stationless bike sharing services also operating in Dublin, mainly in areas not covered by DublinBikes.


Dublin Bus serves Dublin and the Greater Dublin Area, covering the broadest area among all. Services generally run from around 5:30am to 11:30pm, though some 24-hour routes are in operation and Nitelink services are available on weekend nights. You can check out their timetable, or use TFI Live (app version available) to learn about specific operation times for your bus.


Luas

Luas (Irish for 'speed') is Dublin's tram network, with two lines serving the city. The Green Line runs north–south through the city centre, while the Red Line connects the southwest to the Docklands. Luas operates on a frequency-based system, not a fixed timetable. You can check real time information for any stop here


DART

The DART is Dublin's electric train system, one of the fastest ways to move through Dublin city centre and out to other parts of the city, running along the coast of the Irish Sea from Malahide or Howth in North County Dublin southwards as far as Greystones, Co. Wicklow. Commuter rail services extend further beyond the DART, reaching into the surrounding counties.>


Payment Method

Dublin Bus accepts coins only. Exact fare is required and no change is given. Card and contactless payments are not yet available on board.

02 What apps can I use for transport-related information?

TFI Live

TFI Live is the official Transport for Ireland app. It provides real-time departures for Dublin Bus, Go-Ahead Ireland, Bus Éireann, Luas, and Irish Rail, alongside timetables, interactive maps, and accessibility information including wheelchair-accessible stops and services. You can also save your favourite journeys, stops, and timetables for quick access on the go.

TFI Go

TFI Go App allows you to buy tickets for participating Transport for Ireland bus services and participating commercial bus operators directly from your phone. Your ticket is downloaded to the TFI Go App and can be used to travel straight away

03 How accessible is Dublin public transport?

Dublin's public transport is generally accessible, though the level of support varies by mode. TFI Journey Planner shows whether your chosen service is wheelchair accessible.

Dublin Bus uses low-floor, wheelchair-accessible buses on all routes. Each bus has one dedicated wheelchair space, accessible via a ramp at the front doors. Luas provides step-free access at all stops and all trams are wheelchair accessible. DART stations are mostly step and gate free, with the exceptions of Greystones, Killiney and Sutton. Wheelchair ramps and staff assistance are available at most stations.

If you need to book accessibility assistance for any TFI service, advance notice is required.

For full accessibility information across all modes, visit transportforireland.ie/accessibility.

Additionally, Independent Travel Support is a free scheme helping people with disabilities travel confidently across all modes.

04 Is there public transport late at night?

24-hour Routes: Dublin Bus offer fifteen 24-hour services which are in operation seven days a week across ten route: 15, 39a, 41, C1, C2, C5, C6, E1, E2, G1, G2, N4, F1, F2, 80. Full timetables are available at https://www.dublinbus.ie/24-hour-timetables.

Nitelink: Twelve Nitelink bus routes depart from Dublin City Centre every Friday and Saturday night to suburbs all over Dublin: 7n, 29n, 31n, 33n, 42n, 46n, 49n, 69n, 70n, 77n, 84n, 88n. Full timetables are available at .

05 How do I get from Dublin Airport to the city?

Dublin Bus (routes 16, 19, 24, 41): Dublin Bus routes 16, 19, 24 and 41 operate between city centre and the airport. You can pay on the bus in coins or by a Leap Card. These are slower but cheaper options. Dublin Express (routes 782 , 783 , 784): Dublin Express provides three express routes between Dublin Airport and Dublin City Centre. Tickets can be booked through the Dublin Express website or purchased on board by card only (no cash, no Leap Card). Young Adult and Student Leap Card holders can get a 50% discount by booking through the TFI Go app. Aircoach (routes 700 & 700X): The Aircoach service operates up to every 15 minutes between Dublin Airport and Dublin City Centre, continuing onward to Leopardstown. Tickets can be bought on the website, through the Aircoach mobile app, on board, or at the Aircoach departure stand at Terminal 1 (Zone 2) and Terminal 2 (Zone 20). Most services accept both cash and contactless payments on board. Leap Cards are also accepted on Aircoach services for single journey fares.
