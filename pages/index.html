Born on February 2nd 1882, James Joyce is considered one of Ireland’s most celebrated writers for his books, Dubliners and Ulysses. The eldest of 10 children, Joyce lived in Brighton Square, Rathgar with his parents, John Stanislaus Joyce and Mary Jane “May” (née Murray). Joyce attended Clongowes Wood College, Belvedere College and University College, formally located on St. Stephen’s Green, before moving to Paris to study medicine. Joyce returned to Ireland in April 1903 to attend to his family after the death of his mother and stayed until 1904. During this time, he met his future wife Nora Barnacle, from county Galway.

During this time Joyce also wrote his first short story for the magazine Irish Homestead. In October 1904, Joyce and Nora left Ireland to live in Pola, now known as Pula Croatia, where Joyce became an english teacher. After 1904, Joyce only visited Ireland three other times with his last visit being in 1912.
