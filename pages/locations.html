The Sisters | Street

After the 1891 death of Charles Stewart Parnell, a leading Irish Nationalist, the name was changed from “Great Britain” to “Parnell” Street.

It’s a bridge crossing the Royal Canal and is generally identified as Newcomen Bridge, in the North Strand area (Nuimgeography, 2019).

Pigeon House

An Encounter | Historic Building

The Pigeon House was the electricity and power station which served the city of Dublin. It is now a deserted port (O’Connor, 1990).

North Strand Road

An Encounter | Street

North Strand Road is a street in the Northside of Dublin.

Wharf Road

An Encounter | Street

The Wharf Road, now East Wall Road, is a road that runs along the top of the wall built to keep the River Tolka on course on its way to the sea so that it doesn’t flood the city’s northern-eastern neighborhoods and industrial complexes.

North Richmond Street

Araby | Street

North Richmond Street is a quiet cul-de-sac on Dublin’s north side and forms the opening setting of Araby. Joyce uses the street to establish the confined domestic world of the narrator before his journey toward the bazaar begins.

Map It Out

Virtiol Works

An Encounter | Historic Buildings

Vitriol (sulphuric acid) was used for bleaching linen cloth from the mid-18th century onward.

Map It Out

River Liffey

An Encounter, A Little Cloud, A Painful Case | Rivers & Canals

The Liffey is Dublin's main river, cutting through the heart of the city and splitting it into a northern and southern half.

Map It Out

Christian Brothers School

Araby | Historic Buildings

The Christian Brothers’ School on North Richmond Street is mentioned in Araby and also appears in Ivy Day in the Committee Room. It reflects the strong presence of Catholic education in everyday Dublin life during Joyce’s time.

Map It Out

Buckingham Street

An Encounter, Araby | Streets

Buckingham Street lies on the north side of Dublin and forms part of the boy’s route toward the bazaar in Araby. The street helps trace his movement from a quiet residential neighborhood into the wider city.

Map It Out

Westland Row Station

After the Race | Historic Buildings

Westland Row Station appears in both Araby and After the Race as a point of travel and departure. It represents Dublin’s growing modern transport network at the turn of the twentieth century.

Map It Out

Naas Road

After the Race | Streets

Naas Road forms the western approach into Dublin and is mentioned in After the Race. The racing cars travel along this road as they speed toward the city, symbolizing modernity and excitement.

Map It Out

Dame Street

Two Gallants, A Little Cloud, Counterpart | Streets

Dame Street is one of Dublin’s major city-centre streets and a hub of commercial activity. In Two Gallants and A Little Cloud, it forms part of the busy urban landscape through which the characters move.

Map It Out

St Stephen’s Green

Two Gallants, After the Raceste| Parks

St Stephen’s Green is Dublin’s best-known public park and a landmark in the city centre. In Two Gallants, Lenehan passes through the park during his wanderings across Dublin.

Map It Out

Rutland Square

Two Gallants| Park

Rutland Square, now known as Parnell Square, is the first location mentioned in Two Gallants. Its Georgian setting reflects the faded elegance of nineteenth-century Dublin. It was renamed to Parnell Square in 1933 and features the Garden of Rememberance park.

Map It Out

Dorset Street

Two Gallants, Clay | Streets

Dorset Street is a busy northside street in Dublin and appears early in Two Gallants. It forms part of the route taken by the characters as they walk through the city.

Map It Out

Baggot Street

Two Gallants, A Painful Case| Streets

Baggot St. runs southeast from St. Stephen’s Green to the Grand Canal.

Map It Out

Donnybrook Tram

Two Gallants | Historic Buildings

The Donnybrook tram line was launched in 1873, it connected Merion Square to Donnybrook’s southern terminus. The Donnybrook tramway is no longer in operation, but is now used as the Donnybrook Bus Garage.

Map It Out

Trinity College Dublin

After the Race, Two Gallants, The Dead | Historic Buildings

Trinity College was founded in 1592 by Elizabeth I. It was built upon the grounds of All Hallows, a former Augustinian priory that had been dissolved during the Reformation.

Map It Out

Temple Bar

Counterparts | Restaurants & Pubs

Temple Bar is an area on the south bank of the River Liffey in central Dublin, lined with pubs and restaurants. Farrington moves through this zone on his drinking crawl. Today it’s best known as the city’s main tourist and nightlife district.

Map It Out

Shelbourne Road

Counterparts | Street

Shelbourne Road is a “lower-middle-class” residential street in Ballsbridge, connecting Merrion Road toward the Bath Avenue/Haddington Road area. It's where Farrington exits the tram at the end of his exhausting night.

Map It Out

Nelson's Pillar

Clay | Statue

Nelson’s Pillar stood on O’Connell Street and was a major city-centre landmark and meeting point, shaping how people navigated central Dublin. It was destroyed in 1966.

Map It Out

Lucan Road

A Painful Case | Street

The Lucan Road runs westward along the south side of the Liffey, connecting Chapelizod to Lucan, the town to the city. Its westward direction connotes a departure, an escape from the city.

Map It Out

Crowe Street

Counterparts, Grace | Street

Crowe Street is located in the Temple Bar area south of the River Liffey. In Grace, it is the location of Tom Kernan’s workplace in the city.

Map It Out

Suffolk Street

Ivy Day in the Committee Room | Street

Suffolk Street is a short street connecting the Grafton Street area with St Andrew’s Street. In Ivy Day in the Committee Room, it serves as a meeting point within Dublin’s political city centre.

Map It Out

O’Connell Bridge

Counterparts, The Dead, Two Gallants | Bridge

O’Connell Bridge is one of Dublin’s central bridges connecting the north and south sides of the city. It appears in both Counterparts and The Dead, marking an important crossing point in Joyce’s Dublin.

Map It Out

Phoenix Park

The Dead | Parks

A 17th-century, 707-hectare park, created for royal hunting, mentioned as “the Park”, the Parkgate in the Dubliners.

Map It Out

Johnny Rush’s

The Sisters | Pub

Johnny Rush’s is a pub, located on South William St., Historically, it was a real business located near Findlater’s place.

Map It Out

Bewley’s

A Little Cloud | Cafe & Restaurant

A cafe, located in Grafton Street, which was frequented by Irish literary and artistic figures, including James Joyce.

Map It Out

Theatre Royal

The Dead | Theatre

One of three major early 20th-century Dublin theatres, it was primarily used for dramatic presentations. It was demolished in 1962.

Map It Out

Port and Docks

The Dead | Port

Also known as Dublin Port, established in 1707 and was headquartered at different places along the Liffey river.

Map It Out

Queen’s Theatre

A Mother | Theater

The theatre, often called simply the Queen’s Theatre or the Queen’s, was characteristically known as a venue for comic and musical acts. In 1969, the theatre was demolished.

Map It Out

Chapelizod Bridge

A Painful Case | Pub

It is the unnamed pub only distinguished in the story by its location at the Chapelizod bridge, with its connotations of romance and history, that offers Duffy the ability to feel tenderly.

Map It Out

Adam and Eve’s

The Dead | Church

Adam and Eve’s, the Fransiscan church was, from 1889 on, officially the Church of the Immaculate Conception.

Map It Out

Ancient Concert Rooms

A Mother & The Dead | Historic Buildings

The Antient Concert Rooms at 52 Pearse Street was demolished in the mid-20th century. Today, the site has been replaced by office buildings and facilities used by Trinity College Dublin.

Ormond Quay

A Mother | Streets

Ormond Quay is one of many waterfront spans of street lining the Liffey in central Dublin. Situated on the north bank, its upper (western) and lower (eastern) sections are bisected by Capel Street as it becomes Grattan Bridge.

Merrion Street

Two gallants | Streets

In Joyce's era, Merrion Street featured rows of elegant Georgian townhouses constructed following the 1748 completion of Leinster House. The domestic servant Corley manipulates almost certainly worked in one of these affluent households.

Map It Out

Grattan Bridge

Two gallants | Bridge

Grattan Bridge crosses the Liffey and joins Capel Street to Parliament Street; originally known as Essex Bridge before later rebuilds/renaming.

Map It Out

Dublin Castle

Grace | Castle

Situated in the heart of Dublin in Dame Street. A 13th-century structure built on Viking ruins and served as the headquarters of British administration in Ireland for over 700 years.

Map It Out

Shannon Waves

The Dead | River

Refers to River Shannon, located in the west of Ireland , flows southward from the Cuilcagh Mountains in County Cavan to the Atlantic Ocean, passing through 11 counties.

Tivoli Theatre

Counterparts | Theatre

Formerly The Grand Lyric Hall / Lyric Theatre of Varieties, the Tivoli Theatre was located in Burgh Quay on the south bank of the Liffey, was later acquired by the Irish Press newspaper group in 1930.

Kavanagh’s

IVY DAY IN THE COMMITTEE ROOM | Pup

A spot for political, under-the-table deals, located in Parliament Street, between Essex and Dame Streets, south of Liffey.

St Catherine’s Church

The Sisters | Church

St. Catherine’s is a Roman Catholic church on Meath Street, situated around the corner from St. Catherine’s in Thomas Street.

Rutland Square

Two Gallants | Square

Rutland Square is part of the Northside setting. It was renamed Parnell Square in 1933, bordered on northwest and southeast by Palace Row and Parnell Street (formerly known as Great Britain Street)

Earl Street

Two gallants | Streets

Earl Street is located near the Liffey, running between Sackville (O’Connell) Street and Marlborough Street.
